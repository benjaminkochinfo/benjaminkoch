HOW TO USE · WORLD INTELLIGENCE TERMINAL

What is this terminal?

World Monitor is a live world desk in the browser. It brings together public news, markets, weather, disasters, maps, and simple risk scores — so you can see the big picture in one place.

What is it for?

To watch and understand what is happening: wars and calm, food and fuel prices, chips and metals, storms, shipping, and how life may feel cheaper or more expensive (housing, school, groceries, and more). It is built for learning, awareness, and clear thinking — not for giving orders or secret intelligence.

What can you do with it?

  • Pick a desk (Command, Compare, Inflation, Affordability, Power · Net, Chips…)
  • Choose a country and a lens (a simple question)
  • Filter by region (Americas, Europe, APAC…) and developed / developing
  • Read answers, costs, power mix, telecoms, outages, inflation, and weather / travel warnings
  • Layout adapts automatically on desktop, tablet, and phone · Add to Home Screen / UWP
  • Collapse the desk rail or bottom stream for more workspace
  • Follow live headlines and prices, and open the map

Why does that help?

The world is noisy. Putting news, prices, and risks side by side — in plain language — helps you notice connections (for example food prices and storms, or chips and copper) and stay calm with constructive paths, not only bad news.

Privacy. This app does not set cookies and does not save layout, settings, or feed cache on the device. When you close the tab, the session ends. Data is fetched live for this visit only. “Add to Home Screen” uses a tiny install helper only — it does not store feeds or settings for this visit offline. (News sites you open may use their own cookies — that is outside this terminal. The map itself uses no external tiles.)
